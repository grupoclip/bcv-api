BCV dollar, euro and official exchange rates

BCV Today publishes the official Banco Central de Venezuela rate for the US dollar, euro, Chinese yuan, Turkish lira and Russian ruble against the bolivar. You can check the current value, review the daily history or consume the data from a public JSON API.

For searches like BCV dollar price today, BCV exchange rate USD VES today, dolar bcv hoy, bcv dolar or dolartoday bcv, this page shows the official Banco Central de Venezuela rate rather than a parallel-market quote.

How to use BCV Today for the official dollar rate

The homepage summarizes the current official Banco Central de Venezuela rates and links to a dedicated page for each currency. The BCV dollar page shows the official USD/VES price for today, quick conversions from dollars to bolivares, recent historical values and access to the JSON API. It is useful for checking payments, budgets, invoices, internal reports and accounting calculations with an official reference.

The main value is the USD/VES rate: how many bolivares equal one US dollar according to BCV. When the effective date falls on a weekend or holiday, the previous business day's official rate can remain in effect. BCV Today keeps the capture timestamp, the effective date and a daily history so users can review changes over time.

BCV Today does not publish or estimate a parallel dollar rate. Searches such as Dolartoday BCV, bcvhoy, bcv dolar or dolar bcv hoy point here to the official Banco Central de Venezuela reference, alongside other official rates including euro, yuan, Turkish lira and Russian ruble.

Common use cases

The official BCV rate is used daily to convert dollar-denominated prices, estimate amounts in bolivares, review received payments, prepare budgets and document internal operations. For companies and accounting teams, a stable reference helps avoid differences between spreadsheets, administrative systems and financial reports. For developers, the API makes it possible to integrate the current value into dashboards, bots, invoicing systems or automated processes without relying on manual copies.

When comparing sources, always check the effective date and the origin of the data. Banco Central de Venezuela can publish a rate for the next business day, so a rate captured in the afternoon may have a later effective date. BCV Today keeps that information visible so users can distinguish between the update time and the day when the rate officially applies. If you need to automate the same check, the data is available as static JSON files.
